  The Causal Chain (your narrative spine) 
                                                                                                  
  1. The problem is real and massive                                                              
  - 48% of undergrads never ask a single question all semester in large science lectures — primary
   reason: fear of embarrassment, not lack of confusion (Nadile et al., PLOS ONE, 2021)           
  - 70% of undergrads experience classroom communication apprehension (CBE Life Sciences          
  Education, 2018)                                                                                
  - Women are 2.4x more likely than men to never ask questions (same study)                       
                                                                                                  
  2. Existing solutions (office hours) are broken                                                 
  - Only 66.7% of STEM students attend office hours even once; only 52.5% of upperclassmen do     
  (Dolan et al., CBE Life Sciences Education, 2022)                                               
  - 10.9% cite intimidation/social stigma as the barrier — but 26.2% of faculty blame "lack of    
  effort" (same study) — a massive perception gap                                                 
                                                                                                  
  3. U-M already proved anonymity works — on this campus
  - Perry Samson (U-M Professor of Atmospheric Science) introduced anonymous backchannel Q&A in   
  his own large lectures → questions went from ~50/semester to 300–500/semester (6–10x increase)  
  - 97% of students wanted the tool in all large lectures (Inside Higher Ed, EdSurge, Michigan IT,
   2019)                                                                                          
  - Gender gap in participation disappeared with anonymity                                        
  
  4. AI tutoring outperforms the status quo                                                       
  - Harvard RCT (194 students): AI tutoring produced 2x learning gains vs. traditional active
  learning (Kestin et al., Scientific Reports, 2025)                                              
  - RAG-based AI specifically solves the hallucination problem — confirmed across 47 papers (MDPI 
  Applied Sciences, 2025)                                                                         
                                                                                                  
  5. Students are already ready for this
  - 86% of students globally already use AI in their studies; 53% want their institution to       
  provide AI tools (up from 30% last year) (Digital Education Council, 2024)                      
  - U-M enrollment: 52,855 students (record high, Fall 2024) — scale matters

  Primary Research: Survey to Run in Libraries / with TAs
                                                                                                  
  Here's a short survey you can hand out or share as a Google Form to students and TAs at the
  Shapiro/Hatcher libraries:                                                                      
                  
  ---                                                                                             
  LearnPool Student Survey (~3 min)
                                                                                                  
  For Students:
  1. How often do you ask a question out loud in a lecture with 50+ students? (Never / Once or    
  twice / Regularly)                                                                              
  2. What stops you from asking? (Fear of judgment / Don't want to slow class / Could look dumb / 
  Schedule — just ask after / Nothing stops me)                                                   
  3. Have you ever left a lecture confused but didn't ask? (Yes / No)                             
  4. Have you ever wanted to ask a TA something but didn't go to office hours? (Yes / No — if Yes,
   why?)                                                                                          
  5. If you could type a question anonymously during class and get an AI answer based only on your
   course materials, would you use it? (Definitely / Probably / Probably not / Definitely not)    
  6. What's your biggest concern about AI answering course questions? (Accuracy / Not nuanced     
  enough / Prefer human / No concern)                                                         
  7. School/year/course size of your largest current class: (open field)                          
                  
  For TAs:                                                                                        
  1. How many students are in the course(s) you TA?
  2. What % of students do you think come to office hours?                                        
  3. What are the most common reasons students give for not coming?
  4. Do you feel students are getting their questions answered in a timely way during lectures?   
  5. Would a tool that handles routine conceptual questions automatically help you focus office   
  hours on harder problems?
